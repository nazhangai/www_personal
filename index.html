Bio

I am currently a machine learning researcher in the Institute for Fintech Research at Tsinghua University with a focus on developing machine learning methods that can aid high-stakes decision-making. Meanwhile, I am working on Reinforecement Learning (RL) and Trustworthy Machine Learning advised by professor Qiang Liu at UT Austin. My goal is to develop data-efficient and trustworthy machine learning systems that can continuously improve through interaction. Towards this end, my latest efforts explore a number of topics including: (i) improving off-policy evaluation in RL and developing offline RL algorithms that can turn static dataset into sequential decision-making engines, (ii) investigating how to effectively and flexibly incorporate domain knowledge into machine learning, (e.g., via monotonic neural networks), and (iii) creating interpretable machine learning methods to better address the transparency, fairness and accountability issues.

Prior to joining Tsinghua, I was a graduate student at Harvard advised by professors Jeffrey Frankel and Carmen Reinhart. I conducted research at the intersection of finance and machine learning. Before that, my study and work at MIT lied in the broad area of decision-making under uncertainty advised by professor Richard de Neufville.
